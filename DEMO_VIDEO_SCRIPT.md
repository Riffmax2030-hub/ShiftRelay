# ShiftRelay Demo Video

**Target duration:** 2 minutes 35 seconds

Use the Amina → David → Faith → Jordan demo flow. Record in a clean browser window at 1080p if possible. Do not show loading screens, API keys, passwords, or editor windows.

## 0:00–0:15 — The problem

**Show:** ShiftRelay landing page.

**Say:**

> Shift changes are still managed with paper notes, rushed calls, and scattered chat messages. That makes it easy for critical information to get lost between one worker and the next. I built ShiftRelay to make every handover clear, accountable, and easy to act on.

## 0:15–0:30 — Enter the workspace

**Show:** Open **Preview workspace roles**, then choose **Amina Otieno — Operations worker**.

**Say:**

> ShiftRelay is a mobile-first workplace portal for shift-based teams. Each worker sees a focused dashboard with their role, current shift tools, work history, and the next action that matters.

## 0:30–0:55 — Worker handover

**Show:** Amina’s dashboard, then **New handover**. Enter or show a prepared shift update and click **Generate handover**.

**Say:**

> An outgoing worker can write or record a shift update. Instead of passing unstructured notes to the next team, ShiftRelay turns the update into a clear handover for review.

## 0:55–1:20 — GPT-5.6 Sol

**Show:** The generated handover summary, actions, risks, and missing-context questions.

**Say:**

> GPT-5.6 Sol is used server-side to structure the update into a summary, action items, risks, ownership, timing, and missing context. The AI supports the worker, but it does not make the final decision. The supervisor still reviews the handover before it moves forward.

## 1:20–1:42 — Supervisor review

**Show:** Switch role, choose **David Kariuki — Shift supervisor**, open the item waiting for review, complete the checklist, and relay it.

**Say:**

> The supervisor receives the handover in a review queue, checks the important duties, and relays it only when it is complete. This creates a simple and accountable workflow rather than a message that disappears in a group chat.

## 1:42–2:00 — Incoming shift

**Show:** Switch to **Faith Wanjiku — Incoming worker**, open the relayed update, complete the checklist, and acknowledge it.

**Say:**

> The incoming worker receives a focused checklist and acknowledges the handover. Everyone in the workflow can see that the information was passed on and accepted.

## 2:00–2:18 — Workforce tools

**Show:** Amina clocking in or the Calendar, then switch to **Jordan Lee — Organisation owner** and show staff approval, schedule, attendance, or analytics.

**Say:**

> ShiftRelay also gives organisations clock-in and clock-out records, schedules, staff approvals, notifications, open-shift coverage, and workforce visibility without making the worker dashboard feel crowded.

## 2:18–2:35 — Codex and close

**Show:** Return to a clean ShiftRelay dashboard or landing page.

**Say:**

> I used Codex to accelerate the full build: the role-based workflows, PostgreSQL-backed data flows, mobile user experience, performance fixes, and documentation. ShiftRelay helps teams start every shift with the full picture.

## Recording checklist

- Keep the final YouTube video **public** and under 3 minutes.
- Record with your microphone in a quiet place; speak naturally and slowly.
- If a screen takes time to load, stop recording and start again after it finishes.
- Cut all typing, waits, mistakes, and silent gaps.
- Add a simple title card only if it does not take more than 3 seconds.
- Export as MP4, 1080p, H.264 video with AAC audio.
